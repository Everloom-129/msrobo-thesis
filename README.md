## Abstract

> _Placeholder draft — replace with your official abstract._

Robot foundation models — large vision–language–action (VLA) policies trained across diverse tasks and embodiments — are advancing quickly, but our tools for **measuring**, **improving**, and **understanding** them have not kept pace. This thesis tackles all three. On **evaluation**, it asks how to benchmark generalist policies in a way that reflects real deployment rather than narrow lab setups. On **enhancement**, it studies methods that make these policies more capable and reliable. On **explanation**, it opens up the internals of modern VLAs to understand *why* they behave the way they do. Together these threads form a loop: better evaluation surfaces failure modes, those failures motivate enhancements, and interpretability explains what changed and why.
